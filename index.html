The book

The Art of Soulful Work

Not "follow your passion" — a phrase worn smooth into uselessness. Soulful work is the practical integration of what energises you, what you believe in and what you are good at — and this book is the bridge between the job you hold and the work that is actually yours, built plank by plank while you walk on it. Twelve chapters, one method: unearth the core, read your own history for evidence, design the ecosystem, prototype cheaply, settle the money question honestly, and make the whole thing a lifelong practice rather than a one-off escape.

Real people, real crossings

Rachel Power spent fourteen years being told she had "reached her potential" — until the method in this book turned that verdict into a beginning. She is now an Amazon best-selling author (Redefining Career Success), a licensed career coach, and founder and chief executive of Power Your Potential. Her story threads through every chapter, with her blessing, joined by Suzanne Diamond — whose crossing began from a very different rung of the ladder and is told in Chapter Ten. They are offered not as decoration but as evidence: the process works, on real people, with mortgages and doubts and Tuesday meetings.

Both stand behind their stories by name.
